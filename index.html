Welcome to the Stevenson Spring Science Competition.

This is our second annual science competition held at Stevenson HS for its neighboring middle schools. All 6th, 7th, and 8th graders are welcome!

Who runs the event?

The event is Stevenson student run. It is associated primarily with the Stevenson WYSE/TEAMS club. WYSE stands for Worldwide Youth in Science and Engineering; TEAMS stands for Tests in Engineering Aptitude, Math and Science. Both are STEM competitions in which Stevenson students compete. The Spring Science Competition will provide you with a brief, yet meaningful, taste the of science competitions you’ll experience in high school!

What can I expect?

Free food, challenges of intellect and leadership, and a chance to get to know your fellow math and science enthusiasts. Components of the event are both individual-based and team-based; the competition in its entirety focuses on seven important STEM disciplines: Mathematics, Physics, Biology, Computer Science, Chemistry, Engineering Graphics, and English.

What should I bring?

Pencils, a calculator, and a healthy problem-solving attitude. Be prepared to have some fun! We encourage you to sign up with a team. If you come without having registered as part of a team, we will do our best to assign you to a team so that you don’t miss out on the collaborative aspect of the competition.

How do teams work? How do I form a team?

Teams will consist of up to 8 members. Team members will have chances to show their skills individually, and as part of a group. Teams have no lower limit in terms of membership.

When? Where?

May 23, 2015 (tentative) at Adlai E. Stevenson High School. The address of the school is 1 Stevenson Dr. Lincolnshire, Illinois 60069.

Why?

Because we love science, and so do you!
